BIRD Education Platform

Conception and design of a mobile app for Germany's national education platform, making every educational opportunity in Germany discoverable and accessible.

Challenge

How do you make Germany's education landscape navigable?

BIRD (Bildungsraum Digital) is a national education platform funded by the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research. The existing web prototype needed a mobile companion app to help learners not just find programs, but feel guided and supported throughout their educational journey. The target group: primarily high school students encountering the breadth of available paths for the first time.

Process

User Research & Journey Mapping

We mapped the journey of education seekers and identified key moments of uncertainty and overwhelm. Interviews with high school students revealed a need for personalisation and guidance: not just a search engine, but a companion that understands where you are in life.

Defining Features & Priorities

Based on our findings, we defined the core feature set: personalised content via the BIRD Wallet, intelligent search across all programs and institutions, a personal notebook for collecting favourites, and a chatbot offering real-time support. Each feature was mapped to a specific user need.

Wireframes to High-Fidelity

We developed wireframes for all core flows and iterated toward high-fidelity screens. The visual language, inspired by paper and handwriting, was chosen to feel familiar and approachable for younger users, while communicating quality and trustworthiness through texture and tactility.

Usability Testing & Iteration

The interactive prototype was tested with students and educators. Feedback confirmed the value of the personalisation layer and the chatbot, and led to refinements in the notebook and search interactions. The paper-inspired visual style was consistently well-received.

Solution

A companion for every step of the learning journey

The BIRD mobile app lets learners explore every educational program and institution in Germany in one place. Integrated with the BIRD Wallet, it surfaces personalised content based on the user's current education and interests. A personal notebook keeps track of favourites and notes, while an intelligent chatbot answers questions along the way. The paper-inspired visual design creates warmth and trust, particularly for first-time users navigating an overwhelming system.

Impact

Clarity and warmth for a complex system

The prototype was well-received in usability tests with students and educators, praised for its clarity, emotional accessibility, and ease of navigation. The concept is currently under consideration for further development as part of the national BIRD platform strategy.
