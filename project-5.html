LOVEsick

An interactive museum exhibit at Visualiseringscenter C in Sweden, inviting young visitors to explore the biology and feeling of falling in love.

Visualiseringscenter C

Team of 6

Challenge

Making the science of love feel personal and fun

The Visualiseringscenter C in Norrköping, Sweden, invites student teams to develop interactive science exhibits for young audiences. Our brief: explain the complex biology and psychology of falling in love in a way that's engaging, relatable, and safe to explore, even for teenagers who might not even admit they're curious.

01 Discovery

Research and Target Group

We researched the biology and psychology of falling in love and studied how science museums engage young audiences. We mapped the emotions, questions, and knowledge gaps typical of first experiences with romantic feelings.

02 Define

Dual-Sided Narrative Structure

We defined the core narrative: a love-struck patient who needs examination. Two opposing perspectives shaped the exhibit's structure: the left side explores the biological and chemical mechanisms, the right the emotional and sensory experience. Visitors choose their entry point.

03 Design

Tangible Interaction and Roleplay

Visitors become nurses, using a physical tangible as a stethoscope to 'examine' the patient. A doctor narrates the findings, explaining that love is natural and nothing to worry about. The concept transforms a potentially embarrassing topic into something warm, funny, and safe.

04 Test

Watchout Prototype Evaluation

The concept was prototyped in Watchout and evaluated for clarity, engagement, and educational impact. The dual-sided structure proved highly effective in creating curiosity and guiding exploration without prescribing a single path.

Solution & Impact

Put on the nurse's coat and explore

LOVEsick lets visitors step into the role of a nurse, using a tangible stethoscope to examine a fictional patient suffering from love. The left body half answers 'How does it work?', covering hormones, chemistry, and biology. The right side explores 'What does it feel like?', tracing emotions, sensations, and personal experience. A doctor's voiceover guides the examination with warmth and humour, reassuring visitors: this patient has absolutely nothing to worry about.

LOVEsick received outstanding feedback from educators and visitors for its creativity, educational clarity, and emotional resonance. The Visualiseringscenter C selected it as their favourite student project, with potential for real integration into the permanent exhibition. It demonstrates how physical interaction and playful roleplay can turn a complex topic into a memorable learning experience.
