Game Design · Sustainability

Little Food Fighters

A printed card game about food waste designed for families, packaged directly in the grocery bag and extended with an AR companion app.

My Role

Game & Visual Designer

Client

Linköping University
Course Project · Team of 4

Duration

[Duration · Year]

Tasks
Game Design Illustration Print Design AR Implementation
Challenge

Teaching kids about food waste without being preachy

One third of all food produced globally is wasted, and changing that starts at home. The challenge was to design a sustainability intervention for families with children that didn't feel like a lecture. It had to reach kids where they already are: at the table, playing. The game also needed to connect to SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production) in a way that was concrete and age-appropriate, not abstract.

Process

Food Waste Research & Family Games

We researched the scale and causes of food waste, from shopping habits to expiry date confusion, and studied how existing games successfully teach sustainability to younger audiences. This revealed a gap: most interventions target adults, not the family unit as a whole.

Mechanics, Format & SDG 12

We defined the core game mechanics: cooperative play, food-character cards with personalities and vulnerabilities, and a grocery bag distribution format that puts the game directly in families' hands. The link to SDG 12 was embedded in the narrative: players rescue food from waste, making the sustainability message structural rather than instructional.

Illustration, Print & AR App

Each food character was illustrated with a distinct personality, making the cards collectable and emotionally engaging. The print layout was designed for the physical card format and grocery bag packaging. An AR companion app was implemented to extend the game into the digital space, bringing characters to life when scanned.

Playtest with Kids & Parents

The game was playtested with families, children and parents playing together. Observations focused on engagement, rule clarity, and whether the sustainability message landed naturally. The AR feature generated immediate excitement and became a strong motivator for repeat play.

Solution

The game that comes home with your groceries

Little Food Fighters is a cooperative card game where players team up to rescue expiring food characters from the waste bin. Each character, from Broccoli Bob to Leftover Larry, has a personality, a backstory, and an AR alter ego. The game arrives folded inside a grocery bag, meeting families at the exact moment food decisions are made. Play makes the lesson; no lecture required.

Impact

A sustainability lesson disguised as a game

Little Food Fighters showed that behaviour change design doesn't need to be earnest; it needs to be fun. The grocery bag distribution model was particularly well received for its elegance: zero extra effort from the family, maximum reach for the message. The project demonstrated how physical game design and AR can work together to make abstract sustainability goals feel personal and playable.
