The Horror Mode

A Halloween motion piece for Akkodis that turns corporate social media into something genuinely creepy through illustration and animation.

Client

Akkodis

Internal Project · Solo

My Tasks

ConceptIllustrationAfter Effects Animation

Challenge

Standing out in a sea of corporate Halloween posts

Every year, brands flood social media with the same orange-and-black Halloween aesthetics: pumpkins, skeletons, and generic spooky vibes. Akkodis wanted something different: a Halloween post that felt genuinely creative, in-character, and worth stopping for. The brief was open, and the challenge was finding an angle that felt authentic rather than forced. Image rights held by Akkodis.

01 Concept

Finding the Right Angle

Ideation focused on concepts that felt native to Akkodis's identity as a technology and engineering consultancy. Ghost-Centered Design emerged as the idea: a fictional UX framework that reframes haunting as a design problem, using language and formats the audience would immediately recognise.

02 Script

Writing the Framework

The Ghost-Centered Design framework was written out in full: complete with ghost personas, pain points, journey maps, and interaction principles. The script was structured as a short animated presentation, mirroring the format of real UX case studies.

03 Design

Visual Language and Motion

The visual style combined the Akkodis brand with a deliberately eerie aesthetic: desaturated tones, ghostly overlays, and UX diagram conventions applied to supernatural subject matter. Each frame was crafted to be readable as a still image as well as in motion.

04 Animation

Bringing the Framework to Life

The piece was animated and produced for LinkedIn. Timing, pacing, and caption design were optimised for auto-play viewing without sound, ensuring the content landed even for users who scrolled past without audio.

Solution & Impact

Ghost-Centered Design, activated every 31st October

The Horror Mode is an animated short built around the Ghost-Centered Design framework, a fictional design methodology for optimising the haunting experience. Complete with user journey maps, pain points, and interaction principles (all ghost-specific), the piece translates UX language into something absurd and immediately shareable. It gives Akkodis a recognisable creative concept they can revisit each Halloween.

The Horror Mode performed strongly on LinkedIn, outperforming standard Akkodis content in engagement. More importantly, it demonstrated that a tech consultancy can have a genuine personality, one that resonates with the designers, engineers, and creative thinkers they want to attract. Ghost-Centered Design is now a recurring internal concept.
