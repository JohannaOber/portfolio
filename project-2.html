Mobile-First Car UX

A thesis project for Audi AG exploring how personal smart devices can become the core of the Level 5 autonomous driving experience.

Challenge

Rethinking in-car interaction when there's no wheel to hold

In a future where cars drive themselves at SAE Level 5, the entire premise of in-car UX changes. How do we design interactions when driving is no longer the user's task? How can a personal smart device enable a seamless, holistic travel experience that adapts to individual needs, emerging behaviours, and societal shifts? This thesis explores exactly that. Detailed content remains confidential under NDA with Audi AG.

Process

Trend Research & Future Scenarios

Using Speculative Design, I explored and analysed major trends shaping the future of mobility, smart devices, and human behaviour. Multiple plausible future scenarios were developed and evaluated for relevance and impact.

Persona in a Future World

A fictional persona was placed into the most plausible future scenario. Their daily life, needs, and interactions were visualised through artefacts, tangible representations of the future world that formed the foundation for the interaction concept.

Interaction Concept & Prototype

Based on the defined scenario, I developed a forward-looking interaction concept that shifts in-car UX from control-based to experience-based. A prototype was built to make the concept tangible and testable.

Immersive User Study

The prototype was evaluated in a user study using immersive storytelling and scenario-based testing. Participants navigated the future scenario and provided feedback on the concept's usability and desirability.

Solution

From control to experience: a new paradigm for in-car UX

While full details remain confidential, the core concept places the user's personal smart device at the heart of the in-car experience. With autonomy removing the need for manual control, the design shifts focus to personalisation, connectivity, and making travel time meaningful. The result is a holistic travel experience platform built for the passenger, not the driver.

Impact

Sparking strategic conversation at Audi

The project generated valuable internal discussion at Audi AG about how digital ecosystems may evolve in the mobility space. It demonstrated the power of Speculative Design as a tool for long-term UX innovation, and was completed as a solo thesis project.
